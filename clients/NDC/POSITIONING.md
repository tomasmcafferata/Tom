## Positioning Statement

```
FOR:        Marketing Managers and Events Coordinators at Argentine B2B companies
            (20–300 employees) who exhibit at trade shows, operate branded vehicle
            fleets, or manage retail/office physical communications

WHO:        Are juggling 3–5 fragmented vendors — a printer, a stand builder, a
            transport company, a design studio, an installation crew — and losing
            days of productive time coordinating between them before every event

NDC IS:     The only integrated visual communications partner in Argentina that
            designs, produces, and installs stands, fleet wraps, retail signage,
            and full event logistics entirely in-house — from the first render to
            the last vinyl applied on-site

UNLIKE:     Stand specialists (BDG, Argentina Stands, Enterprise Show) who do not
            do fleet or restyling; fleet specialists (Base3) who do not do stands
            or events; and print shops that do not build, install, or manage logistics

NDC:        Gives you one contact, one brief, and one team accountable for the
            entire physical brand experience — so you stop being the coordinator
            and start being the client
```

## The Core Insight

Every competitor in this market has optimized for one vertical. Stand builders build stands. Fleet specialists wrap fleets. Print shops print. The Argentine market has never produced an integrated SME-scale visual communications company — because it's operationally hard to do.

NDC has done it. They have the print plant, the fabrication workshop, the 3D rendering team, the installation crew, and the event logistics capability under one roof. That's the moat. It's not a marketing claim — it's a physical fact that requires 15 years and significant capital investment to replicate.

The positioning opportunity: NDC is not competing on price, turnaround, or quality claims that every competitor also makes. NDC is competing on **integration** — a structural advantage that no competitor currently offers.

## Positioning by Segment

### Against Stand Specialists (BDG, Argentina Stands, Enterprise Show, Irrazabal, UltraKey, Stands Company)

```
THEIR CLAIM:    "Llave en mano" stands, 20+ years, custom design, great track record.
                (Note: every single one uses "llave en mano" — the phrase is dead.)

THE PROBLEM:    "Llave en mano" for them means: we handle the stand.
                NOT the fleet wrap your trucks need for the same event.
                NOT the store restyling happening the same quarter.
                NOT the event logistics if anything goes wrong on setup day.

NDC'S WEDGE:    "¿Y la flota? ¿Y el local? ¿Y la logística del día del evento?"
                NDC is the only stand company in Argentina that can also answer
                yes to those questions — from the same team, on the same brief.

PROOF POINTS:   Own print plant (they print what they build — no quality gap between
                render and reality because the same team controls both)
                15 years of production + installation experience in Argentine venues
                Full event logistics capability (AV, transport, staffing, security)
```

### Against Fleet Specialists (Base3, Aguafuerte)

```
THEIR CLAIM:    Best fleet wrapping in Argentina. Enterprise clients (Quilmes,
                Andreani, Red Bull). Process rigor, end-to-end project management.

THE PROBLEM:    Base3 is excellent — for large enterprises that only need fleet.
                The SME with 10–30 branded vehicles who ALSO exhibits at Expo Rural
                must use Base3 for the fleet and somebody else for the stand.
                Two vendors. Two briefs. Zero guarantee of brand consistency.

NDC'S WEDGE:    Brand consistency across every physical touchpoint — fleet, stand,
                store — from the same design team working from the same brand brief.
                For a company rebranding, that consistency is the whole point.

PROOF POINTS:   Same render-to-production workflow for fleet as for stands
                Multi-category engagements: design the stand in January, wrap the
                fleet in March, restyle the showroom in June — same team throughout
```

### Against Print Shops / Fragmented Suppliers (Gráfica 312, RotulArte, IAM Gráfica, etc.)

```
THEIR CLAIM:    Fast turnaround, great prices, full-format printing capabilities.

THE PROBLEM:    They print. They don't design, build, install, or care what the
                client is trying to achieve. The Marketing Manager still needs to
                manage the relationship, approve the files, coordinate the install.

NDC'S WEDGE:    NDC is not the printer in the chain. NDC replaces the chain.
                One brief in → entire event out.
                No coordinator needed, no files to chase, no install crew to manage.
```

## Messaging Architecture

### Primary Message (for cold outreach)

> "La mayoría de las empresas que exponen en [Feria] coordinan 4 proveedores para un solo stand.
> NDC los reemplaza a todos: render, producción, impresión, logística e instalación — todo en casa."

**Why it works:**
- Opens with a recognized, painful truth (the 4-vendor problem)
- No generic quality claims
- Immediately implies NDC's structural advantage without stating it directly
- Invitation to imagine the alternative

### Secondary Message (for follow-up / LinkedIn)

> "Un brief. Un equipo. Del render al montaje."

**Why it works:**
- Extremely compact — fits a LinkedIn connection note
- "Render" signals quality (they visualize before they build)
- "Montaje" signals end-to-end (they're there on setup day)
- Positions NDC as the one vendor who owns the outcome

### Category-Specific Hooks

```
TRADE SHOW TRIGGER:
  "Vi que [Empresa] va a estar en [Feria] en [Mes].
   Coordinamos el stand completo — render, producción, impresión, instalación —
   para que el día del evento lo único que tengas que hacer sea llegar."

FLEET TRIGGER:
  "Para empresas de logística y transporte, el camión es el primer vendedor.
   NDC se encarga de todo el proceso: diseño, impresión, aplicación — para que
   la flota salga a la calle con la misma cara que el stand."

RESTYLING TRIGGER:
  "Si están abriendo nuevas sucursales o renovando imagen, NDC es el único
   proveedor en Argentina que puede encargarse del local, la flota y el stand
   desde el mismo equipo."
```
