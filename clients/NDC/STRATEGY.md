## Company Overview

NDC Communication Group ("Enedece" — phonetic Spanish for N-D-C) is a privately held, vertically integrated visual communications and event production house founded in 2009, based at Valentín Vergara 3727, Florida Oeste, Greater Buenos Aires. Team size: 2–10 employees. Contact: 11 5263-3258 / info@enedece.com.ar.

The core business model is project-based B2B production: a single client engagement covers some combination of design, fabrication, printing, logistics, and on-site installation — all handled in-house. NDC does not subcontract; that is the product.

## Business Model

```
BUSINESS MODEL
==============

Revenue type:       Project-based (transactional) — quotation-driven
                    No public pricing; scope determines price per engagement
Revenue mix:        Likely: Stands & Events > Ploteo Vehicular > Retail Signage > Other
                    (stands = highest per-project value; fleet = highest recurring potential)

How they win work:  Primarily referral and repeat business (no evidence of active outbound)
                    Zero case studies published → trust built exclusively via word-of-mouth
                    Near-invisible online presence = nearly all growth is relationship-driven

Core advantage:     True vertical integration — 5 service categories under one roof:
                    1. Stands y Eventos (3D render → fabrication → on-site installation)
                    2. Ploteo Vehicular (vinyl printing/cutting → application)
                    3. Arquitectura Publicitaria (corpóreos, backlights, signage structures)
                    4. Restyling de Locales / Sucursales (multi-branch chain rollouts)
                    5. Banners y Textiles Publicitarios (lona, flags, microperforado, merch)

                    + Peripheral services: Digital/Social Media, Diseño Web (underveloped)

Competitive moat:   A competitor can match them on any single category.
                    Nobody in the Argentine SMB market does all five in-house at one price.

Production assets:  Own digital print plant (latest-gen equipment)
                    Own fabrication workshop (custom stand construction)
                    High-fidelity 3D rendering capability
                    Full event logistics capability (AV, transport, staffing)

Deal size signals:  No public pricing. Comparable market data suggests:
                    - Single stands: $150K–$1M ARS
                    - Full stand + production + installation: $300K–$2M ARS
                    - Fleet wrapping (full fleet): $1M–$5M+ ARS
                    - Retail chain rollouts: $500K–$3M ARS per rollout cycle
```

## Brand Voice

```
BRAND VOICE
===========

Current tone:       Warm, partnership-oriented, artisanal
                    "Codo a codo con el cliente" (side-by-side partnership)
                    "Todo en NDC" (one-stop-shop)
                    "La más alta calidad y atención al detalle"
                    "Soluciones para empresas de cualquier tamaño y presupuesto"

What works:         Partnership framing is differentiating — clients buy relief, not prints
                    "Todo en NDC" is the exact right value prop if backed by proof

What's missing:     Zero client stories or named case studies — brand is claims without evidence
                    No founder voice or named team — anonymous company, low trust signal
                    "Cualquier tamaño y presupuesto" signals price flexibility but undercuts
                    premium positioning — the best clients aren't looking for cheap

Recommended voice   Keep: warm, collaborative, outcome-focused
adjustments:        Add: specific outcomes ("el stand se veía exactamente como el render")
                    Add: client industry language (not "ploteo vehicular" — "tu flota, tu marca")
                    Drop: generic quality claims without proof
                    Drop: "cualquier presupuesto" framing when targeting event-season buyers
                    with fixed deadlines (they will pay for certainty, not for cheap)

Tagline candidates: "Un proveedor. Un brief. Todo resuelto."
                    "Del render a la instalación, sin perder el hilo."
                    "Vos llegás al evento. Nosotros llegamos primero."
```

## Growth Vectors

```
GROWTH VECTORS
==============

Vector 1: TRADE SHOW EXHIBITOR OUTREACH (highest immediate ROI)
  Mechanism:    Identify companies registered/announced for the next 2–3 major Argentine
                trade shows. Contact Marketing Manager 60–90 days before the event.
                Offer: full stand package (render + fabrication + logistics + installation).
  Why now:      NDC has no active outbound — this market is finding them by accident.
                A single Tier 1 outreach push of 50 accounts could 3-5x pipeline.
  Timing:       Q2 2026 targets: ExpoEFI (June), Alimentaria Argentina (August),
                Automechanika Buenos Aires (August), FITHEP (September)

Vector 2: FLEET WRAPPING RECURRING CONTRACTS
  Mechanism:    Target logistics / transport companies with 10–100 vehicle fleets.
                Entry offer: 5-vehicle pilot wrap at reduced rate.
                Convert to annual maintenance + new vehicle onboarding contract.
  Why now:      Fleet wrapping is the highest-volume, most recurring revenue category.
                One logistics company with 50 vehicles = repeat work every 2–3 years
                as vehicles cycle. NDC can own this category in GBA with the right outreach.
  Target list:  Logistics companies listed on CEDOL or with branded fleets visible on
                major Buenos Aires transport corridors.

Vector 3: MULTI-BRANCH RETAIL CHAIN ROLLOUTS
  Mechanism:    Target retail chains (10–50 locations) undergoing restyling or expansion.
                Position NDC as the production partner for the rollout — not a printer.
                NDC coordinates across all locations, ensuring brand consistency.
  Why now:      Retail rebranding cycle is 3–5 years. Companies planning a 2026 restyling
                are making vendor decisions now. One chain rollout = 6–12 months of work.
  Signal:       Watch for: "nueva imagen" announcements, franchise system expansions,
                new store opening announcements on LinkedIn / press.

Vector 4: CORPORATE EVENT ANNUAL RETAINER
  Mechanism:    After a first stand engagement, propose an annual retainer:
                NDC manages all physical communications for the year — stands, fleet,
                signage updates, internal events — under one contract.
  Why now:      Currently all work is one-off. A retainer would stabilize revenue and
                dramatically increase LTV per client.
  Target:       Companies attending 3+ trade shows per year (food & bev, pharma, logistics).
```

## Buying Triggers

```
BUYING TRIGGERS
===============

HIGHEST URGENCY (act within 48 hours of spotting signal):
  1. Company announces trade show participation (LinkedIn post, exhibitor list published)
     → Fixed deadline, allocated budget, Marketing Manager is actively planning NOW
  2. New store opening or franchise expansion announced
     → Signage, branding, and restyling budget unlocked immediately
  3. Fleet renewal job posting (fleet manager hired, or news of new vehicle procurement)
     → Wrapping budget is part of vehicle acquisition cycle

HIGH URGENCY (act within 1 week):
  4. Company rebranding / new logo / visual identity announced on LinkedIn or press
     → All physical touchpoints need updating — stand, fleet, retail signage simultaneously
  5. New Marketing Manager or Events Coordinator hired
     → New person wants to establish their vendor roster; open to pitches for first 90 days
  6. Recent funding round or acquisition
     → New budget available, often triggering brand refresh and event presence

SEASONAL TRIGGERS (calendar-driven):
  7. 60–90 days before any major Argentine trade show (see TAM.md for calendar)
     → ProximaFeria search: Expo Rural (July), ExpoEFI (June), Alimentaria (Aug),
        FITHEP (Sep), Automechanika (Aug), Cosméticos & Perfumería (Oct)
```

## Competitive Advantages

```
COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGES
======================

STRUCTURAL (hard to copy):
  ✓ True vertical integration — 5 categories in-house, no subcontracting
    → Competitors handle 1–2 categories; NDC is the only vendor for the whole project
  ✓ Own print plant + own fabrication workshop under same roof
    → No coordination delays between print, build, and install
  ✓ 15+ years operational experience in the Argentine market
    → Knows the venue logistics, local unions, trade show regulations others trip over
  ✓ Full event logistics capability (AV, transport, staffing, security)
    → Most print companies cannot do this; most stand companies subcontract it

OPERATIONAL:
  ✓ 3D renders before production begins — client sees the final result before it's built
    → Reduces revision cycles and last-minute changes at the venue
  ✓ Serves any size and budget — SME entry point to large-corporate delivery quality
    → Low-stakes first project (banner, back de prensa) converts to stand engagements

POSITIONING GAPS TO CLOSE:
  ✗ Zero case studies or named clients online — biggest credibility gap
  ✗ No external trust signals (reviews, press, awards)
  ✗ LinkedIn: 182 followers — functionally invisible to cold prospects
  ✗ No founder/team story — anonymous company, harder to trust for a first project
  ✗ National reach not clearly articulated (implied but not stated)
  ✗ No retainer / recurring model currently marketed
```
