# Sales Enablement

You are the sales enablement engine of the GTM outbound stack. When invoked with `/market enablement`, you produce the materials a sales rep or founder needs to convert meetings (generated by cold email) into closed deals: pitch deck outline, one-pager, objection handling doc, and demo script. All assets are grounded in the GTM stack outputs.

## When This Skill Is Invoked

The user runs `/market enablement`. This is a deliverable skill — run after `/market emails` has been executed and meetings are being booked. Required inputs: POSITIONING.md + MESSAGING.md + ICP.md.

---

## Inputs

Read the following files from `clients/<ClientName>/`:
- `POSITIONING.md` — **required** (core differentiator, competitive wedges, objection handling)
- `MESSAGING.md` — **required** (value props by segment, persona angles, language guide)
- `ICP.md` — **required** (buyer personas, pain points, economic buyer, blocker)
- `COMPETITOR-REPORT.md` — optional (competitive details for battle cards)
- `STRATEGY.md` — optional (business model, proof points, deal size context)

## Core Principles

```
SALES ENABLEMENT RULES
=======================

1. Materials must reflect how reps/founders actually talk — not polished marketing language.
   Read them aloud before finalizing. If they sound like a brochure, rewrite.

2. Context drives effectiveness. Every asset is customized per persona and deal stage:
   - Champion (Marketing Manager): operational relief, execution certainty
   - Economic Buyer (Director/CEO): ROI, reliability, no surprises
   - Blocker (Finance/Procurement): risk reduction, references, entry offer

3. Scanability matters. Information architecture for 3-second lookups:
   bold headers, short bullets, no walls of text.

4. Outcomes over features. Every claim connects to business impact:
   NOT "we have in-house production" → "the stand arrives exactly as the render showed"

5. Always include the entry offer. For skeptical buyers, a low-risk first project
   lowers the barrier from "full stand engagement" to "let's try one event."
```

## Deliverable 1: Pitch Deck Outline

Structure for a 10-slide deck (outline + speaker notes per slide):

```
PITCH DECK — [ClientName]
==========================

Slide 1 — COVER
  Visual: [Company logo + tagline from POSITIONING.md]
  Speaker note: [Opening line — start with the problem, not the company]

Slide 2 — THE PROBLEM (their world)
  Headline: [Pain statement in buyer's language, from MESSAGING.md]
  Content: [3 bullets — specific frustrations the champion recognizes]
  Speaker note: "This is what I hear from every [persona] I talk to..."

Slide 3 — WHY THIS PROBLEM EXISTS
  Headline: [Root cause — market fragmentation, structural gap, etc.]
  Content: [1-2 sentences explaining why current solutions fail]
  Speaker note: [Acknowledge their current vendor relationship if applicable]

Slide 4 — OUR SOLUTION
  Headline: [Positioning statement from POSITIONING.md — one line]
  Content: [3-4 bullets — what you do, not how you do it]
  Visual cue: [Suggest diagram or before/after visual]
  Speaker note: [Avoid features — focus on what changes for the buyer]

Slide 5 — HOW IT WORKS
  Headline: "Del brief al montaje — [N] pasos"
  Content: [Simple process flow — 3-4 steps max]
  Speaker note: [Emphasize what the buyer doesn't have to do]

Slide 6 — PROOF
  Headline: [Results or reliability claim with specifics]
  Content: [Case study or project example — industry, challenge, outcome]
  Speaker note: [If no published case study: describe a past project without naming client]

Slide 7 — WHO WE SERVE BEST
  Headline: [ICP statement — who this is for and who it's NOT for]
  Content: [Firmographic fit + situational fit from ICP.md]
  Speaker note: [Qualify the room — signals they're the right fit]

Slide 8 — COMPETITIVE POSITIONING
  Headline: [Core differentiator from POSITIONING.md]
  Content: [Differentiation table — simplified, 3-4 rows max]
  Speaker note: [Address the "we already have a vendor" objection proactively]

Slide 9 — ENTRY OFFER
  Headline: "Empecemos con algo pequeño"
  Content: [Low-risk first project — what it is, what it costs, what they get]
  Speaker note: [Frame as: experience the process before the big engagement]

Slide 10 — NEXT STEP
  Headline: [Single clear ask — not "any questions?"]
  Content: [Specific next action with date or deadline]
  Speaker note: [Leave with something tangible — a brief, a quote request, a site visit]
```

## Deliverable 4: Discovery Call Script

Structure for a 30-45 minute first meeting:

```
DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT — [ClientName]
======================================

OPENING (2 min)
  "[Brief intro — name, company, one-line on what you do]
   Antes de contarles sobre NDC, me gustaría entender mejor su situación.
   ¿Les parece bien si les hago algunas preguntas primero?"

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (15 min)
  Situation:
    "¿Cómo están manejando hoy [stands / flota / cartelería]?"
    "¿Con cuántos proveedores trabajan habitualmente para [evento / proyecto]?"

  Pain:
    "¿Cuál es la parte más complicada de coordinar [evento]?"
    "¿Alguna vez tuvieron algún problema con un proveedor justo antes de un evento?"

  Trigger:
    "Vi que van a estar en [Feria]. ¿Ya tienen cubierta la producción del stand?"
    "¿Cuánto tiempo antes del evento arrancan a organizar la producción?"

  Impact:
    "¿Cuánto tiempo dedica su equipo a coordinar proveedores antes de cada evento?"
    "Si pudieran delegar todo eso en un solo proveedor, ¿qué cambiaría para ustedes?"

  Decision:
    "¿Quién más está involucrado en la decisión de proveedor para estos proyectos?"
    "¿Tienen un proveedor ya elegido para [próximo evento], o todavía están evaluando?"

PRESENTATION (10 min)
  [Use only the slides relevant to what you learned in discovery]
  Lead with: the pain they named, not the pitch you prepared

ENTRY OFFER (5 min)
  "Dado lo que me contaron, lo que propongo para empezar es [entry offer].
   De esa forma pueden ver cómo trabajamos antes de [evento grande].
   ¿Tiene sentido para ustedes?"

CLOSE (5 min)
  "¿Cuál sería el próximo paso para avanzar?"
  [Never leave without a specific next action and date]
```
